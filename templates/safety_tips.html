Online Safety Tips

Practical advice to stay safe while browsing the internet

Essential URL Safety Tips

URLs are your gateway to websites - learn how to identify safe ones from dangerous ones.

Check for HTTPS

Always look for "https://" at the beginning of website URLs, especially before entering any personal information. The 's' stands for secure.

Verify Domain Names

Pay close attention to the domain name. Phishing sites often use domains that look similar to legitimate ones with slight misspellings.

Beware of URL Shorteners

Short URLs (like bit.ly links) can hide the true destination. Use our URL checker to verify where they lead before clicking.

Look for Security Indicators

Most browsers show a padlock icon for secure sites. Click on it to verify the site's security certificate.

Email Security Tips

Email is a common vector for phishing attacks and malicious URLs.

Be Wary of Unexpected Emails

If you receive an unexpected email with links or attachments, even if it appears to be from someone you know, verify with the sender through another channel before clicking.

  • Never click on links in emails claiming there's a problem with your account
  • Hover over links to preview the URL before clicking
  • Be suspicious of emails with urgent calls to action
  • Check the sender's email address carefully, not just the display name
  • Use our URL checker to verify any suspicious links in emails

General Online Safety Practices

Additional tips to maintain your online security.

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Create complex passwords and never reuse them across multiple sites. Consider using a password manager.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, browser, and security software to patch vulnerabilities.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring a second verification method.

Use Caution on Public Wi-Fi

Avoid accessing sensitive accounts or entering personal information when using public networks.
